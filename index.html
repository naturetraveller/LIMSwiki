What is LIMS?

A Laboratory Information Management System (LIMS) is a comprehensive solution designed to manage all aspects of a laboratory, from sample tracking and data collection to task automation. LIMS systems provide a central platform for managing workflows, reporting, and integrating laboratory instruments while ensuring compliance with regulatory standards and maintaining data integrity.

What is LES?

A Lab Execution System (LES) is a specialized platform for automating and standardizing laboratory workflows and procedures. LES ensures that all laboratory processes are executed consistently and traceably, which is particularly important in regulated environments such as the pharmaceutical industry, where strict adherence to protocols is required.

What is ELN?

An Electronic Lab Notebook (ELN) replaces the traditional paper lab notebook with a digital environment for documenting experiments, observations, and results. ELN systems promote collaboration and make data easily accessible to all stakeholders. ELNs are particularly useful in research environments where large amounts of information need to be securely and efficiently managed.

What is SDMS?

A Scientific Data Management System (SDMS) is designed to store, organize, and analyze large volumes of scientific data. It enables efficient management and processing of unstructured data from various sources and instruments. SDMS systems play a crucial role in managing raw data from experiments and analyses, ensuring that all information is securely stored and easily retrievable.

What is CDS?

The Chromatography Data System (CDS) is a specialized solution for laboratories relying on chromatography techniques such as HPLC or GC. A CDS manages, analyzes, and stores results from chromatography instruments, helping to improve the accuracy and efficiency of sample analysis.
